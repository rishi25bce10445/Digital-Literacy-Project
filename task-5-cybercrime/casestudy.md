#### **Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud**



UPI (Unified Payments Interface) payment fraud is a widespread cybercrime in India where users get tricked into approving fake payment requests. These scams often target students and people who frequently use digital payment apps.



In a typical situation a fraudster approaches the victim pretending to be a buyer or company representative. The fraud usually happens step-by-step.

First, the scammer builds trust by sounding professional and urgent. Second, they use fake links, QR codes or fake request that promise rewards and refunds to deceive users through UPI apps, WhatsApp or SMS. Third, they convinces the victim to enter their UPI PIN, claiming it is needed to “receive money.” However, entering the UPI PIN actually authorizes a payment, and money is transferred from the victim’s account.



Students and new users are particularly at risk since they may not fully understand how UPI systems operate. The consequences of this fraud include financial loss, stress and a decline in trust in digital platforms. In

serious cases, victims may lose their entire bank balance. Recovering lost money is often challenging if the fraud is not reported right away.

This case highlights the importance of understanding digital payment systems and staying alert while using online financial services.
